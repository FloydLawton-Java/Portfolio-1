The first one is:- The Ship of The Desert - Camel!!!!
So why not take a look at the marvellous facts we've got for you here:-
1. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
2. Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat.
3. Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.

The second one is:- Any Guesses? It is the disguise master himself - Chameleon!!!
Lets again take a look at the interesting facts here:-
1. Chameleons have eyes in the backs of their heads.
2. Chameleons have good eyesight for a lizard.
3. Chameleons have extremely powerful tongues.

The third one is:- The Jar fly AKA The Cicada!!!
The interesting facts on Cicadas are mention in here:-
1. Cicadas are like tiny violins.
2. Cicadas make noise like toy frogs.
3. They’re underground builders.

The fourth one is:- The Endangered and Cutest Animal on Earth- Panda!!!!
The interesting facts on pandas are:-
1. Pandas go from pink to white and black (or brown).
2. An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day.
3. A panda Can Poop 28 Kilos per day.

The fifth one is:- One of the most common animal we look in our daily life: Pigeon!!!
The interesting facts on pigeons that you wouldn't know are:-
1. Pigeons have strong muscles used for flying. They can fly at the altitude of 6000 feet.
2. On average, pigeon has 10 000 feathers on the body.
3. Pigeons can move their wings ten times per second and maintain heartbeats at the rate of 600 times per minute, during the period of 16 hours.

The last but not the least:- Our favourite Our own:- Teddy!!!
The little known facts on Teddies are:-
1. The teddy was named after President Theodore Roosevelt, after he refused to shoot a bear during a 1902 hunting trip.
2. They aren’t just for children – 40% of teddy sales are for adults, and a quarter of us still has our childhood companion.
3. A teddy has been into space! Magellan T Bear boarded Space Shuttle Discovery in 1995 as part of a school project.
